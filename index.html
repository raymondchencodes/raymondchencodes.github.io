Can You Read The Problem?

Chicago is the best city in the world. But like any major city, it faces its own set of challenges. One prominent issue is Chicago's severe illiteracy epidemic.

About

Chicago's illiteracy rate is one of the highest in the United States, with a high of over 90 percent. This is due to the high population of Chicagoans, who are primarily from low-income backgrounds. According to the 2023 Chicago Public Schools state report card, 12.2% of low-income third graders were reading at the expected grade level.

Impact!


Illiteracy can lead to poor attendance, academic performance, and high dropout rates.


70% of all incarcerated adults are illiterate and cannot read at a fourth-grade level.


Illiteracy can make it difficult to participate in society and government, leading to feelings of isolation and social exclusion. It can also affect a person's self-esteem, emotional and behavioral skills, and peer connections.


Illiteracy can lead to poverty, unemployment, and lower-quality jobs. People who are illiterate may have difficulty reading instructions, operating machinery, or managing finances, which can make it hard to secure well-paying jobs and financial stability.
